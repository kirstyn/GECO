Genomic surveillance

What is genomic surveillance?

The use of sequencing technologies to generate the genetic code of a disease causing pathogen, typically from diagnostic specimens collected from infected patients. The resulting information adds value to traditional surveillance data.

Why is useful?

Genomic surveillance can be used to identify crucial details about the spread of infection and the changing biology of an infectious agent, which cannot be assessed from epidemiological information alone. For example, genomic information can often be used to identify who transmitted infection to whom which can be especially useful in nosocomial settings. For transboundary diseases, during outbreaks of emerging diseases or reintroductions of previously controlled diseases, genomic data can be used to identify the geographic source of infection and critically genomic data is the only way to identify the cause of an entirely new disease. For pathogens that are changing rapidly, genomic surveillance is essential to monitor drug resistance or changes in virulence that are needed to ensure effective treatments are used.
